*Stegen van Harlingen* is an installation in the medieval alleyways (stegen) of Harlingen, a small harbour city in the north of the Netherlands. Small labels are embedded between the bricks of the alley walls, each one naming something — a material, a measurement, a relation. Plumb bobs hang at intervals along the passage, marking verticals that cross the horizontal of the street.

The work makes visible the system of the city — its structure, its materials, its invisible order — by inserting a parallel system of annotation into the physical fabric of the place.
