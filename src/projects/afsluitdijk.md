A photographic series made on the Afsluitdijk — the 32-kilometre dam that closed off the Zuiderzee from the North Sea in 1932, creating the IJsselmeer. The dam is pure system: a single straight line imposed on water, dividing salt from fresh, sea from lake, old from new. The photographs trace this line, its materials, its light, its weather.
