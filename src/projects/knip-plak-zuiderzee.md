All the houses, sheds, barns and bridges in the Zuiderzee outdoor museum once stood in a village or a city near the Zuiderzee. Between 1966 and 1985 they were relocated, brick by brick, beam by beam, to Enkhuizen — cut from their original context and pasted into a new one. *Knip & Plak* (Cut & Paste) makes this act of displacement visible: each building photographed, labelled, re-ordered. A system of objects torn from one system and placed in another.
